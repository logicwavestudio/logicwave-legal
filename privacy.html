Privacy Policy — ScanFlow

Effective date: 4 May 2026 · Last updated: 2 June 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how the ScanFlow mobile application (the “App”), developed by LogicWave Studio (“we”, “us”), collects, uses and protects your information.

Contact: logicwavestudio@gmail.com.

1. Summary

  • Your scans are stored locally on your device. We do not operate any backend server and do not collect your scans, usage data, or personal information.
  • To help you label products, the App can look up a scanned barcode on Open Food Facts, a free public product database. This sends only the barcode number. The feature is on by default and can be turned off in Settings → Scanning (see section 3.5).
  • Other optional features (Google Drive backup, Google Sign-In, ads, in-app purchases) involve Google services and are governed by Google’s privacy policy in addition to this one.

2. Data we process

2.1 Camera

The App accesses the device camera only to scan barcodes and QR codes in real time. Camera frames are processed locally on your device by Google ML Kit. No image is ever uploaded, stored, or transmitted by the App.

2.2 Scan history (local)

The barcode values you scan, together with optional metadata you choose to add (name, notes, tags, folder, quantity, timestamp), are stored locally on your device in a SQLite database. They never leave your device unless you explicitly enable Google Drive backup (section 3.2).

2.3 Settings

App preferences (theme, language, scan mode, retention period for the trash bin, etc.) are stored locally via Android SharedPreferences. They contain no personally identifiable information.

3. Optional features that involve third parties

3.1 Google Sign-In

If you choose to sign in with Google, the App receives your Google account’s display name, email address, profile photo URL and an internal account identifier. These are cached locally to display your profile in the App. Sign-in is required only to enable the optional Google Drive backup; the App is fully functional without it.

3.2 Google Drive backup (opt-in)

If you enable backup, the App uploads an encrypted snapshot of your scans, folders and recent exports to your own Google Drive, using the OAuth scope drive.file. This scope is restricted by Google so the App can only access files it created (specifically the ScanFlow folder and a single scanflow_backup.bin file). The App cannot read or modify any other files in your Drive. Backups are encrypted with a key derived from your Google account identifier (AES-256-GCM); without your account, the backup cannot be decrypted.

We do not have access to your Drive. Backups live entirely in your Google account. You can disable sync at any time and delete the backup from within the App (Settings → Delete backup) or directly from Drive.

3.3 Google AdMob (advertising)

Free users see banner and interstitial ads served by Google AdMob. AdMob may collect:

  • your device’s Advertising ID;
  • basic device and app information (device model, OS version, app package, country, language);
  • ad interactions (impressions, clicks).

AdMob acts as an independent data controller for this data. See Google’s Advertising policies and Google’s Privacy Policy.

In the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom and Switzerland, the App presents you with a consent prompt via Google’s User Messaging Platform (UMP) before any personalized ad is served. You can withdraw or update your consent at any time from the App settings.

3.4 In-app purchases (ScanFlow Pro)

Subscriptions and one-off purchases are processed by Google Play Billing. We never see your payment data. The App receives only the entitlement state (active / canceled / expired) needed to unlock Pro features. Subscription management (cancel, refund, payment method) happens entirely on Google Play. See Google Play’s terms.

3.5 Product information lookup (Open Food Facts)

To auto-fill a product’s name, brand, quantity and image when you scan or add a barcode, the App can query Open Food Facts, a free and open product database. Only the barcode number is sent, via an HTTPS request of the form /api/v2/product/<barcode>.json; no account, device identifier, location or other personal data is transmitted. The product details returned (name, brand, quantity and an image URL) are stored locally with your entry. This feature is enabled by default and can be turned off at any time in Settings → Scanning → Automatic product info; when it is off, the App performs no product lookups. Open Food Facts is an independent service governed by its own privacy policy.

4. What we do NOT do

  • We do not run any analytics or telemetry SDK.
  • We do not collect device location.
  • We do not access your contacts, messages, photos or files outside our own export folder.
  • We do not share or sell your data for advertising or profiling. Apart from the data handled by the Google services above and the barcode sent to Open Food Facts for product lookups (section 3.5), your content stays on your device or in your own Google account.

5. Permissions

The App requests only the CAMERA runtime permission, used solely for scanning. You can revoke it at any time from Android’s app settings; the App will continue to function for everything except live scanning. The App also declares the INTERNET permission, used for the optional online features above (product lookup, Drive backup, Google Sign-In, ads and in-app purchases).

6. Children

ScanFlow is not directed to children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly process data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with information, contact us at the email above and we will delete it.

7. Your rights (GDPR / similar regulations)

Because we don’t store your data on our servers, most data subject rights are exercised directly on your device: the App lets you delete any scan, empty the bin, clear your history, disable backup, sign out, and delete the remote backup. To exercise rights about data processed by Google AdMob or Google Play Billing, please refer to Google’s privacy controls.

For any privacy-related question, write to logicwavestudio@gmail.com. We aim to respond within 30 days.

8. Data retention

Local data is retained until you delete it or uninstall the App. Items moved to the trash bin are automatically deleted after the retention period you configure (default: 30 days). The Google Drive backup is retained until you disable sync or delete it manually.

9. Security

Drive backups are encrypted client-side with AES-256-GCM before upload. Local data on the device is protected by Android’s standard app sandbox; we recommend enabling a device lock screen for additional protection.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy occasionally to reflect new features or legal requirements. The “Last updated” date at the top of this page always reflects the latest version. Material changes will be communicated through an in-app notice.
